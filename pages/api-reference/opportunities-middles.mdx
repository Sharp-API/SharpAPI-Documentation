# Middles

Find middle opportunities where two sportsbooks have different lines, creating a gap where both bets can win.

```
GET /api/v1/opportunities/middles
```

## Authentication

Requires API key. **Pro tier or higher required.**

## Query Parameters

| Parameter | Type | Default | Description |
|-----------|------|---------|-------------|
| `sport` | string | all | Filter by sport (e.g., `basketball`, `football`) |
| `league` | string | all | Filter by league (e.g., `nba`, `nfl`, `nhl`) |
| `min_size` | number | none | Minimum middle size in points (e.g., `1.5`) |
| `limit` | integer | 50 | Results per page (max 200) |
| `offset` | integer | 0 | Pagination offset |

## What Are Middles?

A **middle** occurs when two sportsbooks offer different lines on the same market, creating a numerical gap where **both bets can win simultaneously**.

Unlike arbitrage (which guarantees profit), middles give you a chance to win both sides of a bet if the final result lands in the "middle" of the two lines.

### Spread Middle Example

```
DraftKings:  Celtics -5.5  @ -110
BetMGM:      Lakers  +7.5  @ -108
```

You bet:
- **Celtics -5.5** at DraftKings
- **Lakers +7.5** at BetMGM

**Possible outcomes:**

| Celtics Win By | Celtics -5.5 | Lakers +7.5 | Result |
|----------------|--------------|-------------|--------|
| 1-5 points | Lose | **Win** | Split (small loss) |
| **6 or 7 points** | **Win** | **Win** | **MIDDLE HIT** |
| 8+ points | **Win** | Lose | Split (small loss) |

If the Celtics win by exactly 6 or 7 points, **both bets win** and you collect on both sides. This is the "middle."

### Total (Over/Under) Middle Example

```
FanDuel:   Over 47.5   @ -110
Caesars:   Under 49.5  @ -105
```

If the total score lands on **48 or 49**, both bets win. You hit the middle.

## Why Middles Are Valuable

1. **Both sides can win** - Unlike arbitrage where you guarantee a small profit, middles offer the chance for a large payout
2. **Limited downside** - When the middle does not hit, you typically lose only the vig (the difference in juice between the two bets)
3. **Larger middles are more likely to hit** - A 2-point middle has roughly twice the probability of hitting compared to a 1-point middle
4. **They exist because books disagree** - Different sportsbooks set different lines based on their risk exposure

## Middle Size Guide

| Middle Size | Quality | Typical Markets |
|-------------|---------|-----------------|
| 0.5 points | Narrow | Rare opportunities, low hit rate |
| 1.0 points | Standard | Common in spreads and totals |
| 1.5 - 2.0 points | Good | Look for these actively |
| 2.5+ points | Excellent | Uncommon but highly profitable |

<Callout type="info">
Use `min_size=1` to filter out narrow middles. Larger middles have a higher probability of landing in the gap, making them more consistently profitable over time.
</Callout>

## Risk Considerations

<Callout type="warning">
Middles are **not risk-free** like arbitrage. When the result falls outside the middle, you lose the vig on the losing side (typically 4-5% of one leg). However, when a middle hits, you win both bets, which more than compensates for the losses over time.
</Callout>

### Expected Value of a Middle

The profitability of a middle depends on:

1. **Middle size** - How many outcomes land in the gap
2. **Probability distribution** - How likely the result is to fall in that range
3. **Juice paid** - The vig on both legs combined
4. **Payout ratio** - Both legs pay roughly 1:1, so hitting the middle pays approximately 2x

For NBA spreads with a 2-point middle, the probability of landing exactly in the gap is typically 8-12%, which often provides positive expected value after accounting for the vig paid on both sides.

## Best Practices

1. **Focus on larger middles** - Set `min_size` to at least 1.0 for better hit rates
2. **Spreads and totals are best** - These markets have the most line variation between books
3. **Track your hit rate** - Log results to verify long-term profitability
4. **Combine with arbitrage** - Some middles also contain an embedded arb (when the combined vig is less than the middle probability)
5. **Act quickly** - Lines converge as books adjust, closing the middle window
